                    Chicken in Red Chile Pipian Sauce

Recipe By     :
Serving Size  : 4     Preparation Time :0:00
Categories    : 

  Amount  Measure       Ingredient -- Preparation Method
--------  ------------  --------------------------------
  3 1/2         pounds  chicken -- cut into serving pieces, loose skin and fat discarded
  4               cups  water
  1           teaspoon  salt
  1 1/2           cups  ripe tomato -- chopped
     1/2           cup  tomatillo -- chopped
  1                     pasilla chile -- stemmed and seeded
  1                     guajullo chile -- stemmed and seeded
     1/4           cup  fresh lime juice
     1/2           cup  sesame seeds
  1         tablespoon  pepitas
  1              stick  1" cinnamon -- broken up
  2          teaspoons  chile caribe
  1                     3" slice of french bread
     1/4           cup  chicken broth
     1/4      teaspoon  achiote paste
  1         tablespoon  all-purpose flour
                        pepitas and sesame seeds for garnish

In a skillet, cook the chicken in 3 cups of the water and the salt over medium heat for 30 minutes. Remove the chicken, keep warm, and reserve the broth for the sauce.

In the empty skillet, combine the tomatoes, tomatillios, chiles, 1 cup of water and lime juice and cook over medium heat for 10 minutes.

IN a dry skillet over low heat, toast the sesame seeds, squash seeds, cinnamon stick and crushed chile, stirring occasionally, for about 10 minutes.

Cut the bread into cubes, and moisten with chicken broth. Set aside.

In a food processor or blender, process the toasted ingredients. Add the cooked tomato mixture and process to make a smooth paste. Add the bread, achiote, 2 cups of the reserved chicken broth, and flour and process until smooth. Return to skillet and heat through.

Place the cooked chicken on a platter and cover with the red sauce. Sprinkle the seeds over the top.
