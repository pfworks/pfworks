             Southwestern Lime Chicken with Ancho Chile Sauce

Recipe By     :
Serving Size  : 8     Preparation Time :0:00
Categories    : 

  Amount  Measure       Ingredient -- Preparation Method
--------  ------------  --------------------------------
     1/2           cup  fresh lime juice
  6        tablespoons  soy sauce
     1/4           cup  vegetable oil
  2        tablespoons  sugar
  2        tablespoons  fresh oregano -- chopped
  1         tablespoon  fresh rosemary -- chopped
  1         tablespoon  garlic -- minced
  1 1/2      teaspoons  chile powder
     1/2      teaspoon  cayenne
  8                     skinless boneless chicken breast half
  8             slices  monterey jack cheese
                        Ancho Chile Sauce

Commbine first 9 ingredients in medium bowl; whisk to blend. Place chicken in a 13x9x2 inch glass baking dish. Pour marinade over. Cover and refrigerate overnight, turning occasionally.

Prepare BBQ. Remove chicken from marinade. Grill chicken until just cooked through, turning, about 10 minutes. Place 1 cheese slice atop each breast half, cover bbq and cook until cheese melts, about 2 minutes. Transfer to a plate and serve with ancho chile sauce.

                            Ancho Chile Sauce

Recipe By     :
Serving Size  : 0     Preparation Time :0:00
Categories    : 

  Amount  Measure       Ingredient -- Preparation Method
--------  ------------  --------------------------------
  2               cups  very hot water
  3                     dried ancho chiles -- stemmed, seeded and torn
  3              large  fresh poblano chiles
     1/2           cup  whipping cream
  1         tablespoon  honey
  2          teaspoons  red wine vinegar

Combine 2 cups hot water and ancho chiles in medium bowl. Let stand until chiles soften, about 30 minutes. Drain, reserving soaking liquid.

Meanwhile, char poblano chiles directly over gas flame or in broiler until blackened on all sides. Enclose in paper bag; let stand 10 minutes. Peel, seed and coarsely chop chiles.

Place drained ancho chiles, 1/2 cup reserved soaking liquid, chopped poblano chiles, cream, honey and vinegar in blender. Puree until smooth, adding more soaking liquid by tablespoons if sauce is too thick. Season to tast with salt and pepper.

Yield:
  "1 1/2 cups"
