                             Jalepeno Steaks

Recipe By     :
Serving Size  : 0     Preparation Time :0:00
Categories    : 

  Amount  Measure       Ingredient -- Preparation Method
--------  ------------  --------------------------------
  1         tablespoon  olive oil
  3        tablespoons  jalepeno pepper -- seeded and thinly sliced
  1                cup  shallot -- finely chopped
  2        tablespoons  garlic -- chopped
     1/2      teaspoon  cumin seed
     2/3           cup  pepper jack cheese -- grated
  2        tablespoons  cilantro -- chopped
  6                     center cut beef felet mignons (8 oz each)
                        freshly ground black pepper
                        poblano chile sauce
  2        tablespoons  olive oil
  1                cup  onion -- chopped
  1 1/2           cups  tomatillos -- husked and chopped
  1         tablespoon  garlic -- minced
  1           teaspoon  serrano pepper -- seeded and minced
  3               cups  poblano pepper -- seeded and chopped
     1/2      teaspoon  coriander seed
     1/2      teaspoon  cumin seed
  1 1/2           cups  chicken stock
  1                cup  medium dry sherry
     1/2           cup  cilantro leaves -- loosely packed
     1/2           cup  toasted almonds or pepitas
                        salt and freshly ground pepper
                        fresh lime juice

Heat the olive oil in a saute pan set over med-high heat until hot but not smoking. Add the jalepenos, shallots, garlic and cumin seed and saute until soft but not brown. Cool and stir in cheese and cilantro.

Slice pockets into the steaks and stuff with jalepeno mixture.Fasten pocket with skewer or tie. Season outside with salt and pepper. Grill until med-rare. Spoon warm sauce onto plates and top with steak.

Sauce: Heat the olive oil in a saucepan set over med-high heat until hot but not smoking. Add the onions, tomatillos and garlic and saute for 3-5 minutes or until lightly colored. Add chiles, coriander, cumin, stock and sherry and bring to a boil. Lower heat and simmer for 5-8 minutes, uncovered until somewhat thickened. Place in food processor, add cilantro and nuts and season to taste with salt, pepper and lime juice.
